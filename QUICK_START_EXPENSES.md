# Quick Start: Using the Expense Tracker

## Adding Your First Expense

1. **Look for "Expense Tracker"** in the left sidebar (below the main parameters)
2. **Click "Add Expense"** button
3. **Fill in the form**:
   - Name: What is this expense? (e.g., "Groceries")
   - Amount: How much? (e.g., 150)
   - Frequency: How often? (weekly/fortnightly/monthly)
   - Category: What type? (food, housing, utilities, etc.)
4. **Click "Add Expense"**

## Using Templates

1. Click **"Add Expense"**
2. Click **"Use Template"**
3. Select a common expense (Rent, Groceries, Utilities, etc.)
4. Adjust the amount
5. Click **"Add Expense"**

## Managing Expenses

### Enable/Disable
- Click the **checkbox** next to any expense
- Disabled expenses are grayed out but not deleted
- Great for seasonal expenses or "what-if" scenarios

### Edit
- Click the **pencil icon** on any expense
- Update any field
- Click **"Update Expense"**

### Delete
- Click the **trash icon** on any expense
- Confirm deletion
- Expense is permanently removed

## Tips

1. **Start with templates** - They cover most common expenses
2. **Use categories** - Makes it easier to see where money goes
3. **Be specific** - "Netflix" is better than "Streaming"
4. **Disable, don't delete** - Keep expenses for future reference
5. **Check the total** - Make sure it matches your actual spending

## Seeing Results

Once you add expenses:
- The simulation **automatically re-runs**
- Check the **timeline table** for cash flow impact
- Look for **negative cash flow warnings**
- Adjust expenses if needed
